TOPIC: Academic Stress and Exam Anxiety


Definition: Academic stress is the body's response to the demands of studying, assignments, and examinations. 


Normalizing Stress: It is completely normal to feel stressed before exams; mild stress can actually improve focus and motivation. 


Signs of Stress: Common signs include difficulty concentrating, forgetting material, restlessness, irritability, and physical symptoms like headaches or stomach upset. 


The Pomodoro Technique: Break study material into 25-minute chunks followed by a 5-minute break to maintain focus.

TOPIC: Depression and Low Mood in StudentsDefinition: 
Depression is a medical condition affecting thoughts, feelings, and daily activities—it is not a sign of weakness. 
Student Context: In India, students often face depression due to academic pressure, leaving home, and uncertainty about the future. 
Symptoms: Persistent sadness (2+ weeks), loss of interest in hobbies, fatigue, and feelings of worthlessness. 
Behavioral Activation: When mood is low, try one small "win" like a 10-minute walk or calling a friend. 
Routine: Restoring a consistent wake/sleep cycle helps the brain fight depressive symptoms.

TOPIC: Anxiety and Grounding Techniques


General Anxiety: Feeling of worry or unease about uncertain outcomes. 


5-4-3-2-1 Grounding: To stop overthinking, name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, and 1 you taste. 

Worry Time: Schedule 15 minutes a day as "designated worry time." If worries pop up earlier, write them down for later. 


Box Breathing: Inhale for 4, hold for 4, exhale for 4, hold for 4. Repeat 4 times to calm the nervous system.

TOPIC: Loneliness and Social Connection


Understanding Loneliness: It is common for students to feel lonely even in a crowd; it often stems from social comparison or big life transitions. 

Social Media Trap: Scrolling through others' "highlight reels" worsens feelings of isolation. 


Action Steps: Join a campus club or volunteer; shared activities make starting conversations much easier.

TOPIC: Sleep Hygiene for Better Grades


The Blue Light Rule: Avoid phones and laptops for at least 30 minutes before bed as they suppress sleep hormones. 


Bed Association: Do not study in bed; your brain needs to associate the bed only with rest. 


The 20-Minute Rule: If you can't sleep after 20 minutes, get up and do a calm activity (like reading) until you feel sleepy.
